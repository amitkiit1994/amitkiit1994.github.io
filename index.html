What happens in a Microsoft Technology Consulting Interview if you are a SDET?

To start with, the first challenge is getting your resume shortlisted and your first call from the recruiter ...
My Interview Experience with Microsoft!

To start with, the first challenge is getting your resume shortlisted and your first call from the recruiter. I am pretty frank here and the toughest part of getting into big techs is getting a call from them, but let me tell you this that shouldn't stop you from applying. I have more than thirty job applications on amazon that are in the archived state before I got a call from them.

In the case of Microsoft, I was approached by a Microsoft employee who had a strong feeling about my LinkedIn profile, he had a gut feeling about me eventually he approached me to check if I was looking for a job in SDET and willing to work for Microsoft. Trust me, I wasn't looking for a change at that time it's because I had recently switched from SAS to JIO and it was only three months since the switch but at the same time I couldn't say no to the opportunity. So that's how it all started but little did I know all of the fun was about to be two months long,

So as soon as I nodded yes the employee from Microsoft referred me and my job application was processed, I was excited to start the interview process and started my preparations for it. I had always heard of Microsoft interviews and I was scared by reading all the leet code reviews. Trust me reading those interview experiences I was only getting demotivated, but also in the back of my mind, I kept my spirits high and was praying for luck to favor me. It's true that no matter how much genius you are and how well you are prepared if luck doesn't favor you then you are eventually gonna fail but it's also true that luck favors only those who do honest hard work and trust me I worked really hard for the interview. 

So the good part about luck favoring me was the time I had for preparations was about a month-long. It started with an initial intimation from the recruiter where I was required to put in my preferred dates of interview, this was the first communication after my referral was processed and this took around a week after the referral happened. After this, I was pretty sure that my resume was shortlisted and the next thing will be the scary interviews. So I waited for about a week, then I received an online assessment link.

Online Assessment

The online assessment was full of ISQTB questions of foundation level and it had one coding question it was a simple array question.

 Once done with the online assessment, I was confident about my answers and I started preparing for the next round which was technical.  So I figured out that the waiting period must be one week within the interviews, but I was wrong, I kept waiting weeks after weeks but there was no revert from the recruiter. I got puzzled, confused, anxious, I mailed the recruitment team, I went back to the referrer, I tried contacting every way possible but I was not getting a satisfactory reply and I couldn't gulp the fact that I was rejected in the online assessment, by the third week I lost all the hope but through these days I never stopped preparing, and eventually, by the end of the third week I got a call from one of the recruiters and I got my first round scheduled.

Techincal Round -1

In technical round 1, I was interviewed by a Consultant where he gave me some three-four coding questions revolving around strings, arrays, hashmaps, etc.  The questions are mostly from GFG, its related to building magic arrays out of a pre-existing array, subarray out of a given array with criteria, sub sequenced string out of the given string with criteria, some sliding window techniques, etc. There were no graphs, trees, etc. Then he asked me questions related to testing fundamentals, like how to design a document for exit criteria, entry criteria, bug lifecycle, etc. Then came the core part, he asked me to design ten end-to-end test cases for youtube which was a good method to evaluate testing skills. In the end, all questions were related to automation tools like API testing, Selenium, Frameworks, etc.

Once done with the tech 1 round, I was pretty confident that I would be clearing it because I was happy with my performance. So again I resumed my preparations and after a week I received a call from the recruiter and got the technical 2 round scheduled.

Techincal Round -2

It's similar to the technical 1 round, as usual, he was asking me some series of coding questions which were easy not too tough but yes you have to put your mind through it to solve it at the place. Then he asked me some testing fundamentals, and then he gave me a scenario and wanted to me write 100% testing coverage test cases for it with a testing technique. I couldn't do it, so he asked me what are testing techniques? I followed with boundary testing and then he suggested using decision testing. I couldn't do it so he explained what is decision testing and how to approach it, I understood it and was able to come up with some satisfactory test cases. Then in the end he asked me to write some complex xpaths and selenium tool related questions.

Trust me, I was not sure about this round since my performance was kind of okayish. So I was scared but still expecting a positive result, and it happened so my luck was by my side and after a week I got a call and was able to schedule my Technical Handson Round.

Technical Hands-on Round

In this round, I was asked to keep a pre-setup ready with all the tools required for automation. It was 2 hour-long interview, I was scared to death because automating UI is kind of a risky job because you are confident that it will run but it doesn't run smooth. I hate UI automation, I was praying if I get an API automation scenario but just as so the opposite happened, I was given 3-4 test cases of one scenario to automate from UI for a Microsoft web product and a medium level coding question which was related to finding the combination of strings. I tried automating the scenario and solved the coding question but guess what the automation was partially executing and most of the test cases for the coding question were failing. So nothing was working fine within that 2hours span, but I had a hell of a lot of robust good quality code which I was proud of. Eventually in the end when there was code pairing with the interviewer, he asked me to solve some problems and change some logic and some exceptions to handle which I was able to come up with. Then he asked me to send the code to him.

I was devastated after it was over. I couldn't believe that my dream was getting faded. None of the things worked as I expected it to work. Eventually that day afternoon I couldn't wait for revert and I made the call to the recruiter and I couldn't believe what she had to say. She said 'Yes'. Trust me I couldn't stop crying after I dropped the call, I cried out loud because it happened and I had a final round with the Hiring manager scheduled that day evening.

Hiring Manager Round

This is the easiest one, just management questions. All though there was one question which I had to speak about a little longer I still remember the question "Is Kubernetes similar to listeners interface?" I gave a lot of theories and possibilities and was diplomatic throughout but in the end, I went for No. I still don't know what should have been the correct answer but doesn't matter, its because I knew the intent of the question was to evaluate if my concepts about Kubernetes is strong and I knew I was strong in Kubernetes its because whenever I read about or learn a new skill I try to read the architecture diagram because that's how you can have your fundamentals clear.

After two weeks I got a call from the recruiter to send me the documents. So this how it all happened.

My biggest Downfall in Life

At the beginning of my career, I owned the notion of working really hard with minimal leaves will result in great work opportunities. Work-life balance just did not exist for me.

Got an offer from SAS.

Maa was so happy and excited when I shared this news. She couldn't believe I got such an exclusive offer from a reputed organization. She was ecstatic and extremely proud of me, I couldn't help but immediately decided to bring her to my place as soon as I join the new organization.

24-04-2019:

Uncle: You need to come to Kolkata right now!!

Uncle: I don't know how to say this - maa is no more.

That's it, a phone call changed my life. I was devastated, I was in shock for a week. I did not know what is happening and how to overcome this. I always used to talk to her all the time about everything. My whole world revolved around her, my joy, sorrow, everything I used to share with her. I could not feel anything emotionally.

After a while, it struck me, What about the switch? that is still pending to deal with. I was dwelling on whether I should switch because I wouldn't be able to perform due to the depression and hardship I was dealing whereas if I stay, I would have time to cope up with the tragic reality, people may understand even if I mess up. A gazillion amount of emotions and questions were clouding my mind yet no answers.

Another thought that dawned upon me was that I know I couldn't perform or do anything, my conscience cease to exist, everything was at pause for me now but the world is not going to stop for me or anyone, it just moves on despite whatever is happening around. Hence the phrase 'Business As Usual.

So I decided and made the switch and it turned out to be one of the best decisions in my life.

My journey within SAS transformed me into a different person, the kind of people I worked with, the mindset, it changed me as a whole. I grew technically, professionally, and in all aspects. All of this was the result of the right decision and the wonderful team I was working with.

I finally understood that we are going to face a lot of hardships in life, but we should not consider it as an excuse to step back instead we should take the risk, get it together, have self-belief, and step forward. Trust me, we are going to bounce back stronger than before.

Work-life balance is an important thing. Acknowledge your loved ones before your work. Do not take them for granted, invest your time with your family, create memories with them because your work is going to stay, and remember, you are always replaceable!

What is the hardest part of Job hunting?

-> Preparations - No

-> Interviews - Maybe

-> Getting shortlisted - Yes! Definitely Yes.

How to get shortlisted?

1. Use resume keyword scanners: Yes, this is your first step, pick your latest resume, run a scan against your desired job description. Figure out the mismatch in skills and update accordingly.

2. Referrals: Yes, it is one of the approaches but you should refrain from requesting referrals like "Please refer me to your company." Instead, I suggest taking your time, identify the job requirements and job IDs you find yourself fit for, prepare a cover letter as to why you think you are a suitable match, help out the referrer with the job id, strike a conversation with the referrer where you pitch yourself the best way possible, Gain trust.

Why am I saying this? You have worked hard in your preparations, you just have to put that last mile of effort.

3. Approaching Recruiters/Hiring Managers: This is one of the best ways. But the question is do you stand out from the crowd? Identify the job requirements and their IDs you find yourself a fit for, start showing your proof of concepts/personal projects. Prepare a cover letter stating how you are the best fit, be presentable, engage in a good conversation, and build trust.

Do It Today!

+ Note down the skills you have now and update your resume accordingly.

+ Set a reminder on your calendar 3 months from today and label it as 'Skill Evaluation'.

+ After 3 months, evaluate your learning & skills you acquired within this period and then, update your resume again.

+ Compare both the resumes.

You will be amazed to find out how much you have grown in 3 months like working/exploring new technology, new domain, new Project, etc.

Do this activity on a recurring basis!

Develop a habit of updating your resume every once in a while to evaluate your progress. Resumes may not only be utilized for applying for jobs, you can use them for self-assessment too.

How do you think you should start job hunting?

+ Search for Jobs you want to apply for, and carefully go through the job descriptions. Identify the skills and technologies that are required, you will find that most of the things are similar across other job positions too. Understanding and upskilling to trending industry requirements is the most important aspect of job hunting.

+ Reserve time for the next 1-2 months, to go through all the techs and skills that you are missing. Once you have gained ample amount of knowledge, try to implement these as a part of POCs to your current project or maybe create personal projects so that you can showcase them to hiring managers.

+ Revise your already known skills thoroughly! Get your fundamentals right! In interviews, you might not want to say that 'I don't have that idea because other teams were handling that part', you might invest time in knowing every aspect of engineering the software goes through.

+ Voila, Go for Job Applications!

Note: This doesn't mean, you shouldn't go for interviews in the course of preparing because you never know when the time is right. Having this mentality to be interview ready always is the best thing, it's because opportunities may come by chance you don't wanna lose them. Always take the risk, there are two sides to risk either positive or negative, but the best part is you are gaining experience and thats gonna remain constant throughout your journey.

Maa was so happy and excited when I shared this news. She couldn't believe I got such an exclusive offer from a reputed organization. She was ecstatic and extremely proud of me, I couldn't help but immediately decided to bring her to my place as soon as I join the new organization.

24-04-2019:

Uncle: You need to come to Kolkata right now!!

Me: Why? What happened?

Uncle: I don't know how to say this - maa is no more.

Me: What? How? Where is she?

Uncle: It's cardiac arrest, we lost her.

That's it, a phone call changed my life. I was devastated, I was in shock for a week. I did not know what is happening and how to overcome this. I always used to talk to her all the time about everything. My whole world revolved around her, my joy, sorrow, everything I used to share with her. I could not feel anything emotionally.

After a while, it struck me, What about the switch? that is still pending to deal with. I was dwelling on whether I should switch because I wouldn't be able to perform due to the depression and hardship I was dealing whereas if I stay, I would have time to cope up with the tragic reality, people may understand even if I mess up. A gazillion amount of emotions and questions were clouding my mind yet no answers.

Another thought that dawned upon me was that I know I couldn't perform or do anything, my conscience cease to exist, everything was at pause for me now but the world is not going to stop for me or anyone, it just moves on despite whatever is happening around. Hence the phrase 'Business As Usual.

So I decided and made the switch and it turned out to be one of the best decisions in my life.

My journey within SAS transformed me into a different person, the kind of people I worked with, the mindset, it changed me as a whole. I grew technically, professionally, and in all aspects. All of this was the result of the right decision and the wonderful team I was working with.

I finally understood that we are going to face a lot of hardships in life, but we should not consider it as an excuse to step back instead we should take the risk, get it together, have self-belief, and step forward. Trust me, we are going to bounce back stronger than before.

Work-life balance is an important thing. Acknowledge your loved ones before your work. Do not take them for granted, invest your time with your family, create memories with them because your work is going to stay, and remember, you are always replaceable!

The time is never right, you can keep on waiting for the time to be right, but that day will never come. Instead, I suggest you do it right now because now is the time.

I got my first job offer through on-campus placements and I was hired by a mass recruiter.

So when I started thinking about a switch, that was around 2 years in corporate life. I started preparing and giving interviews. Even with a good prep (at least I thought so), I was failing every Interview in the first round itself and I continued losing hope thinking "Will I ever be able to crack any interview?"

After a while, I started writing down all the questions that were asked during the interview and kept the question bank updated whenever I was asked any new questions.

Guess what, that note still works wonders for me!

I know you will find a lot of interview questions out there online and you can easily get the most asked ones, but still, I recommend after every interview, make a note of every question that was asked, additionally I also keep track of how I answered and how the experience was. Trust me this will help in the long run.

Why should you track?

Firstly, it will help you in your future interviews and then think about this, your life is a journey, and every individual will have different experiences in different situations. So how wonderful will it be when you look back and find how you were and how much you have grown now?

You are always a better version today than you were yesterday.

It's not about taking the right decision or right path, it's about turning the decision or path into right.

Not always though, a lot of wise thinking needs to be done before deciding but once decided there's no looking back, you turn the decision in your favor and narrate your story.

We all have a common skill - Problem Solving.

Every time you face a challenge in life, you work for it real hard and overcome it. Have you ever wondered? How much do you exercise your mind to solve the problem? How much time and effort did you put in dedicated to cross that mark? How many hits and trials do you make? How do you think about negative scenarios?

I believe we all are solving algorithms every second in our life. So can I say- for software engineers, Problem Solving is programmatically projecting those thoughts or algorithms in a code? Now, if you think you are not good at problem-solving. Think again, the only thing you are missing is a routine practice.

I suggest from today, don't let DSA scare you instead try solving 2-3 problems daily because DSA will make things easier for you. Start with the easiest ones, let your mind get trained and gradually you will be able to solve difficult ones easily. It's because you just need practice for what you already do every day!

You are closer to your goals than you expect. Keep your spirits high!
